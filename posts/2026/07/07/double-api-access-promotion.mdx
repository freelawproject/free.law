<p className="lead">The law belongs to everyone, and so do the tools to understand it. For the next 30 days, we're doubling the API access in every CourtListener membership, so more people can put one of the most powerful tools we've built for that idea to work. If you're a member, your access is already doubled. There's nothing to sign up for.</p>

## Why we're doing this

In May, we made it possible to use CourtListener directly inside AI assistants like [Claude][mcp], so you can research a question, check whether a citation is real, or watch a court for new filings, all in plain conversation. It has been more popular than we expected, and it's one of the most direct routes we've found to putting real legal research in the hands of anyone who needs it, not just the people who can afford an expensive subscription. We want more people to find it, so for a month we're doubling the fuel that powers it.

## What's happening

From July 7 through August 6, the API access in your CourtListener membership is doubled. Tier 1 gets Tier 2 levels of access, Tier 2 gets double, and so on, all the way up. Group members see the same boost. It applies automatically, and it's free.

When the 30 days are up, access returns to its normal level. If you've come to rely on the extra room, you can keep it by [upgrading your membership][membership].

Never connected CourtListener to an AI assistant? It takes about a minute, and [our wiki][wiki] walks through the setup and some prompts worth trying, for Claude and other assistants alike. It's a good month to give it a real workout.

Open legal research only matters if people can actually reach it. For the next 30 days, twice as many of you can.
