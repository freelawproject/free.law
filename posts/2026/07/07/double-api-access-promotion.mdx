<p className="lead">The law belongs to everyone, and so do the tools to understand it. For the next 30 days, we're doubling the API access in every CourtListener membership, so more people can put one of the most powerful tools we've built for that idea to work. If you're a member, your access is already doubled. There's nothing to sign up for.</p>

## Why we're doing this

In May, we made it possible to use CourtListener directly inside AI assistants like [Claude][mcp]. Instead of the AI guessing at the law (and sometimes confidently making things up), it can look things up in our database of real court cases, dockets, and citations, and answer you with the actual record in hand. You can ask it to research a legal question, explain what's happening in a case, check whether a citation is real, or watch a court for new rulings, all in plain conversation.

It is one of the most direct routes we have ever found to the work we exist to do: putting real legal research in the hands of anyone who needs it, not just the people who can afford an expensive subscription. People are using it to understand their own legal situations, to do work that used to require a paid research platform, and to do it in minutes instead of hours. It has been more popular than we expected.

So we want as many people as possible to find it and try it. Doubling everyone's API access for a month is how.

## What's happening

From July 7 through August 6, the API access that comes with your CourtListener membership is doubled. Tier 1 gets Tier 2 levels of access, Tier 2 gets double, and so on, all the way up. Group members see the same boost. It applies automatically, and it's free.

When the 30 days are up, access returns to its normal level for your tier. If you find you've come to rely on the extra room, you can keep it by [upgrading your membership][membership].

## What "API access" actually is

Here's the part that matters, even if the words "API access" make your eyes glaze over.

Think of it as the fuel for everything above. Every time the AI looks something up in CourtListener on your behalf, it uses a little of your API access. The more access you have, the more the AI can look up for you, meaning deeper research, bigger questions, and more back-and-forth before it has to pause and wait. Doubling your access doubles how far you can push it, so it's a good month to give it a real workout.

## How to make the most of it

If you've never connected CourtListener to an AI assistant, this is the time. It takes about a minute:

1. Open [Claude][claude] on the web, desktop, or your phone.
2. Go to "Customize" > "Connectors" > "Browse Connectors."
3. Find CourtListener and add it.
4. Sign in with your CourtListener account.
5. Start asking questions.

Then try a few things and see how far you can go:

- "What's the status of [a case you're following], and what happens next?"
- "Find recent decisions on [a legal issue] and explain them to me like I'm not a lawyer."
- "Watch [a court or case] and alert me when something new is filed."
- "Check every citation in this document and flag anything that looks wrong."

Connecting to other AI assistants works too. Instructions are on [our wiki][wiki].

## After the promotion

We hope you spend the month discovering how useful this can be. If you do, the simplest way to keep the extra headroom is to [upgrade to the next tier][membership]. And if you're not a member yet, [joining][membership] is the way in: every membership includes API access, and right now, that access is doubled.

Open legal research only matters if people can actually reach it. For the next 30 days, twice as many of you can.
