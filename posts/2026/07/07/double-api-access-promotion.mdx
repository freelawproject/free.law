<p className="lead">The law belongs to everyone and our mission is to help people understand it. Free Law Project members support this mission and to thank them we are doubling what they can do with our new AI tool for the next 30 days.</p> 

In May, we released [a tool that merges][mcp] the power of CourtListener with AI tools like Claude and ChatGPT. By installing our new tool, you can do incredibly powerful legal research in plain language. You tell the AI what to do, and it gets to work using the CourtListener database and features.

We've been in awe of how popular this combination is, and we want folks to give it a try. To do that, we want to showcase the power of this new tool by doubling its power for the next 30 days. Our hope is that this will encourage people to give it a try and experience the most powerful tool we've ever built. 

If you're a member, your access is now doubled, and if you're not, we've doubled the free tier too. Either way, there's nothing to sign up for.

## Why we're doing this

In May, we made it possible to use CourtListener directly inside AI assistants like [Claude][mcp], so you can research a question, check whether a citation is real, or watch a court for new filings, all in plain conversation. It has been more popular than we expected, and it's one of the most direct routes we've found to putting real legal research in the hands of anyone who needs it, not just the people who can afford an expensive subscription. We want more people to find it, so for a month we're doubling the fuel that powers it.

## What's happening

From July 7 through August 6, we're doubling API access across the board. Whatever membership tier you're on, your own allotment doubles, from Tier 1 all the way up, and group members see the same boost. We're doubling the free tier too, so even without a membership, anyone with a CourtListener account can do twice as much for the month. EDU and LSO memberships are not part of this promotion. It applies automatically.

When the 30 days are up, access returns to its normal level. If you've come to rely on the extra room, you can keep it by [upgrading your membership][membership].

Never connected CourtListener to an AI assistant? It takes about a minute, and [our wiki][wiki] walks through the setup and some prompts worth trying, for Claude and other assistants alike. It's a good month to give it a real workout.

Open legal research only matters if people can actually reach it. For the next 30 days, twice as many of you can.
