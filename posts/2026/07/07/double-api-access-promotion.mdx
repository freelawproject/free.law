<p className="lead">For the next 30 days, we're doubling the API access included in CourtListener memberships. We're doing this to There's nothing to sign up for and nothing to do. If you're a member, your access is already doubled.</p>

We're doing this for one reason: we want more people to try what is quickly becoming one of our favorite things we've ever built.

## What's happening

From **July 7 through August 6**, the API access that comes with your CourtListener membership is doubled. Tier 1 gets Tier 2 levels of access, Tier 2 gets double, and so on, all the way up. Group members see the same boost. It applies automatically, and it's free.

When the 30 days are up, access returns to its normal level for your tier. If you find you've come to rely on the extra room, you can keep it by [upgrading your membership][membership].

## Why "API access" is worth getting excited about

Here's the part that matters, even if the words "API access" make your eyes glaze over.

A couple of months ago, we made it possible to use CourtListener directly inside AI assistants like [Claude][mcp]. Instead of the AI guessing at the law (and sometimes confidently making things up), it can now look things up in our database of real court cases, dockets, and citations, and answer you with the actual record in hand. You can ask it to research a legal question, explain what's happening in a case, check whether a citation is real, or keep an eye on a court for new rulings, all in plain conversation.

This has been more popular than we ever expected. People are using it to understand their own legal situations, to do work that used to require an expensive subscription, and to do it in minutes instead of hours.

So where does API access come in? Think of it as the fuel for all of that. Every time the AI looks something up in CourtListener on your behalf, it uses a little bit of your API access. The more access you have, the more the AI can look up for you: deeper research, bigger questions, more back-and-forth, before it has to pause and wait.

**Doubling your access means doubling how far you can push it.** It's a great month to give it a real workout.

## How to make the most of it

If you've never connected CourtListener to an AI assistant, this is the time. It takes about a minute:

1. Open [Claude][claude] on the web, desktop, or your phone.
2. Go to "Customize" > "Connectors" > "Browse Connectors."
3. Find CourtListener and add it.
4. Sign in with your CourtListener account.
5. Start asking questions.

Then try a few things and see how far you can go:

- "What's the status of [a case you're following], and what happens next?"
- "Find recent decisions on [a legal issue] and explain them to me like I'm not a lawyer."
- "Watch [a court or case] and alert me when something new is filed."
- "Check every citation in this document and flag anything that looks wrong."

Connecting to other AI assistants works too. Instructions are on [our wiki][wiki].

## After the promotion

We hope you spend the month discovering how useful this can be. If you do, the simplest way to keep the extra headroom is to [upgrade to the next tier][membership]. And if you're not a member yet, [joining][membership] is the way in: every membership includes API access, and right now, that access is doubled.

The law belongs to everyone, and so do the tools to understand it. For the next 30 days, you've got twice as many.
